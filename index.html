Ooma B2B Referral Platform

Executive Decision Brief — Portal A vs Portal B
Prepared: March 12, 2026 Classification: Internal — Leadership Decision Required: Team Funding for Mass Adoption
Context

The Problem

Our AT&T field agents generate warm leads for Ooma VOIP products when internet/phone service is unserviceable at a business address. These leads are handed off to a separate closer team who call the customer, close the deal, and manage onboarding.

Two internal teams independently built software to manage this referral-to-close pipeline. We need to select one team to fund for company-wide rollout.

Overview

The Two Solutions

Portal A — "It's Money Time"

A closer's power tool

Pairs a Google Form for lead intake with a standalone CRM that excels at motivating sales reps — featuring leaderboards, goal tracking, real-time KPI counters, auto-calculated customer pricing, timezone-aware calling, and an energizing UI built for the sales floor.

Gap: Referrer has zero visibility after submitting a lead. No audit trail. No in-app calling or messaging. Google Sheets as data backbone.

Portal B — "Referrals"

An operational platform

A single unified application where referrers and closers log in with role-based access. Referrers get full pipeline visibility with SLA alerts. Closers get click-to-call, in-app email/SMS, and intelligent lead prioritization. Every action is logged in an audit trail.

Gap: Lacks motivational features, analytics depth, and sales-culture energy that Portal A delivers.

Key Insight

The Core Tradeoff

Portal A built the better sales tool. Portal B built the better platform.

Portal A understands what makes closers productive — gamification, competition, and speed. But it was built for one team's workflow and will be difficult to scale. The Google Sheet foundation will break under volume, the PIN-based login is not secure for company-wide deployment, and the referrer side is a dead end.

Portal B understands what makes an operation sustainable — transparency, accountability, and structure. It's built on modern infrastructure that supports multiple offices, roles, and programs. But closers may find it sterile compared to Portal A's energy, and the lack of gamification could impact adoption on the sales floor.

Recommendation

Fund Portal B's Team. Bring Portal A's Team in as UX Consultants.

1

Portal B's foundation is harder to replicate. Proper authentication, a unified database, role-based access, audit trails, SLA monitoring, and multi-channel communication are infrastructure-level features. Retrofitting these into Portal A would take 8–10 months. Adding Portal A's features onto Portal B takes roughly 5 months.

2

Referrer visibility is a business requirement, not a nice-to-have. If referrers can't see what happens to their leads, they lose trust in the process and stop generating quality referrals. Portal B solves this today. Portal A doesn't address it at all.

3

Scalability matters for mass adoption. Portal A's Google Sheet backbone has hard limits (~100K rows) and no multi-tenant support. Portal B's database-driven architecture can support dozens of offices and thousands of leads without degradation.

4

Portal A's magic is in the UX, not the infrastructure. The leaderboards, KPI counters, goal tracking, cost calculator, and motivational design are valuable — but they are frontend features that can be built on top of any solid backend. Portal A's team should shape the closer experience in the unified product.

Risk Management

Risks to Mitigate

⚠️ If Funding Portal B

Ensure closer adoption by mandating that gamification, leaderboards, and motivational elements are in the launch scope — not deferred. Closers will reject a tool that feels like a project management app. Involve Portal A's team in UX design reviews from day one.

ℹ️ If Funding Portal A Instead

Be prepared for a longer timeline, higher cost, and significant architectural rework. The Google Sheet dependency must be fully eliminated before mass rollout. Referrer-side features will need to be built from the ground up.

Portal B is the safer, faster, and more cost-effective foundation for mass adoption.
Portal A's strengths should be absorbed as high-priority feature requirements.

The goal: a platform with Portal B's operational rigor and Portal A's sales-floor energy —
a tool that managers trust and closers love.
