Section 1

Executive Summary

Two teams independently built solutions to manage the AT&T → Ooma B2B referral pipeline, where field agents (Referrers/Setters) generate warm leads for phone-based closers.

Portal A — "It's Money Time"

Hybrid: Google Form + Sheet + Closer CRM

A Google Form for lead submission feeding a Google Sheet, paired with a dedicated, feature-rich Closer CRM with PIN-based login, gamification, and deep sales analytics.

Portal B — "Referrals"

Unified role-based platform

A single web application where both Setters and Closers log in with role-based views, built-in multi-channel communication, SLA tracking, and full audit trails.

Section 3

Referrer / Setter Experience

3.1 Lead Submission

Portal A — Google Form ("OOMA Order Form")

The referrer submits leads through a standalone Google Form with 20+ fields. The form is thorough, with inline pricing, dropdown selectors for phone quantities (0–20), and a full 10-point customer expectation script that the rep must confirm they read aloud. Office/dealer code selection covers 35+ offices. Compliance is enforced through three mandatory checkboxes.

Portal B — In-App Form ("Submit New Referral")

The referrer submits leads through an integrated form within the same platform. Fields are similar but streamlined — the rep is already identified by login, and the address field includes geocoding. Compliance checkboxes are condensed into three items with summarized expectations. Product configuration uses free-text input rather than constrained dropdowns.

3.2 Referrer Visibility & Dashboard

Portal A — None

Once a referrer submits the Google Form, they have zero visibility into what happens next. No dashboard, no status tracking, no feedback loop. The only way to check is to ask the closer team directly.

Portal B — Full Dashboard with SLA Alerts

Comprehensive dashboard showing: total referrals, active/closed/unclaimed/stale counts, claim rate (66.7%), avg time to claim (11.7h), SLA alerts for unclaimed >24h and untouched >48h leads, full pipeline view, and complete audit trail.

Section 4

Closer Experience

4.1 Dashboard & Lead Management

Portal A — "It's Money Time" CRM

Dashboard organized into four sections: Upcoming Appointments (scheduled calls), Fresh Leads (unclaimed with "Up For Grabs" button), Reloop (464 leads needing re-contact, sortable), and Priority (flagged leads). Right sidebar shows real-time KPI counters. Search covers company, name, phone, rep, and email.

Portal B — Unified Work Console

Two views: "Command" (action-priority queue with Suggested Next, Fresh & Unclaimed, Mine – In Progress, Unclaimed – In Progress, Closed) and "Status" (pipeline stages). The "Suggested Next" feature provides intelligent lead prioritization. Dashboard includes Closer Execution metrics (claim rate, time to first touch, touches/referral avg).

Section 5

Analytics & Reporting

Portal A — Dedicated Analytics Page

Full "Extensions & Touch Analytics" page with: date range presets, Quick Look metrics (Total Extensions, Avg Per Sale, Sales Count), four performance cards (Response Speed, Touches Per Sale, Volume, Lead to Sale Complete), Rep Time Sheet, Rep Leaderboard, Top Extensions, and All Filtered Sales. Settings includes weekly Extension Goals with daily/monthly/yearly projections.

Portal B — Role-Based Dashboard Metrics

Analytics embedded in dashboards: Setters see "Setter Health" (sourced total, claim rate, avg time to claim, unclaimed >24h, untouched >48h) with SLA Alerts. Closers see "Closer Execution" (received total, claim rate, avg time to first touch, touches/referral avg, stale needs touch). Admin offers CSV export with configurable filters.

Section 6

Personalization & Settings

Portal A — Extensive Customization

Light/Dark mode, primary color (5 presets + custom), accent text, pill text colors with live preview, lead status colors (Fresh/Mid/Done), appointment defaults, persistent filter defaults, editable text templates, motivational login page with daily quote.

Portal B — Minimal Customization

No theme or appearance customization. Clean, consistent design focused on functional configuration (relationships, ICLs, export settings) rather than aesthetics.

Section 7

Administration

Portal A — Limited

No visible admin panel. User management handled outside the app. Payroll page exists but was empty. Settings are per-user only.

Portal B — Structured Admin Panel

Manager-only Admin page with three tabs: Relationships (setter-closer pairings), ICLs (organizations), and Export (CSV with filters for client, status, date, notes, and history). Role-restricted — closers cannot see Admin.

Section 9

Strengths & Weaknesses

Portal A

✅ Strengths

  • Exceptional closer CRM with gamification
  • Rich analytics with leaderboard & time sheet
  • Pre-built templates ready immediately
  • Auto-calculated monthly cost breakdown
  • Customer timezone with live local time
  • Extensive UI personalization
  • Structured call dispositions
  • Large reloop management (464+ leads)

❌ Weaknesses

  • Zero referrer visibility post-submission
  • No audit trail or accountability
  • No in-app communication tools
  • Google Sheet data dependency
  • No admin panel or role-based access
  • No SLA monitoring
  • Quick Text buttons non-functional
  • No data export from CRM

Portal B

✅ Strengths

  • Unified platform (single source of truth)
  • Full referrer visibility + SLA alerts
  • Complete audit trail with timestamps
  • Multi-channel comms (Call, Email, SMS, Note)
  • Click-to-call integration
  • "Suggested Next" intelligent prioritization
  • Role-based access control
  • Admin panel with CSV export

❌ Weaknesses

  • No auto cost calculator
  • No customer timezone display
  • No gamification or leaderboard
  • No structured call dispositions
  • Templates require setup (empty by default)
  • No dedicated analytics page
  • No goal setting or time sheet
  • No theme customization

Section 14

Final Recommendation

Build on Portal B's foundation and layer in Portal A's closer-experience features.

Portal B provides the architectural backbone that is hardest to build from scratch: proper authentication, a unified database, role-based access, audit trails, SLA monitoring, and multi-channel communication. These are infrastructure-level features that would take 6+ months to retrofit into Portal A.

Portal A's strengths — gamification, analytics, cost calculator, timezone display, structured dispositions, and motivational UX — are primarily frontend features and business logic that can be layered on top of Portal B's foundation in approximately 4–5 months.

The unified platform should carry Portal A's energy and sales-floor culture combined with Portal B's operational rigor and cross-team transparency.

The goal: a platform with Portal B's operational rigor and Portal A's sales-floor energy —
a tool that managers trust and closers love.
